## Hook

If you've ever walked through the Ulta in Cranston and thought "I could run this place," well, now's your shot. The company is actively hiring a Sales Manager, and the pay range is $26 to $34 an hour. That's not nothing.

## What's going on

Ulta Beauty is looking for someone to lead the sales floor team at its Cranston location. We're talking Beauty Advisors, Lead Cashiers, the whole crew. The person in this role owns the retail side of the store — sales numbers, guest experience, scheduling, inventory, the works.

They want 2 to 3 years of retail management experience and a bachelor's degree is preferred, though not required. You'll need to be comfortable reading financial reports, coaching staff, and yes, occasionally doing makeup demos on customers. It's a full-time gig with nights, weekends, and holidays in the mix. So if you were hoping to keep your Sunday mornings free, maybe keep looking.

The pay tops out around $34 an hour plus bonus eligibility, and full-time employees get health, dental, and vision benefits.

## Why it matters

Retail management jobs at this pay level are actually meaningful for the local economy. We're not talking minimum wage work here. A $30-an-hour retail manager in Cranston is a person who can afford rent, maybe a car payment, and still grab a coffee without doing math first.

Rhode Island's retail sector took hits it hasn't fully recovered from, and positions like this one represent stable, community-based employment. Cranston has a solid commercial corridor, and when anchor stores like Ulta invest in quality local management, it tends to keep those shopping areas functioning. That matters for the surrounding small businesses too.

It also signals that Ulta sees Cranston as a market worth investing in, which isn't something to gloss over.

## The Rhode Rash Take

Look, this is a legitimate job at a real company that pays decently for Rhode Island retail. It's not glamorous in the way that "beauty industry" might sound, but managing a team, hitting sales targets, and keeping a store running smoothly is actual work that requires actual skill.

If you've got retail management experience and you've been waiting for something with a little more structure and better pay than what's out there, this is worth a look. Just know what you're getting into: flexible scheduling in retail speak means you will work holidays. That's just how it goes.

But $34 an hour with benefits in Cranston? There are worse ways to spend a Tuesday.
